Degree & Certificate Programs

The Polytechnic@UofM will offer programs designed to enhance workforce preparedness. These condensed curricula (30–40 credits) feature alternative math requirements and flexible electives, allowing students to pair technical programs with other majors. Additionally, credits can be earned through transfers, certifications, and real-world experiences.

Degree Programs (launching Fall 2026)

Polytechnic@UofM will introduce the following degree programs:

B.S. in Information Technology

Degree Program

A workforce-focused program in networking, systems, and applied IT that prepares students to support modern digital operations in sectors such as logistics, healthcare, and public service.

B.S. in Manufacturing Supervision

Degree Program

Prepares students for supervisory and leadership roles in advanced manufacturing environments, with emphasis on operations, quality, safety, and team management.

B.S. in Construction Leadership

Degree Program

Focuses on project coordination, scheduling, safety, and stakeholder communication for construction projects, connecting classroom learning to real-world job sites.

Certificate Programs

Additionally, two certificate programs will be available to students across the university:

Manufacturing Leadership

Certificate Program

Gain practical knowledge in supervisory management, lean concepts, quality analysis, and production control.

Career Opportunities

  • Operations / Manufacturing Supervision
  • Quality Assurance and Control
  • Continuous Improvement and Lean Management

Courses

  • TECH 4381 – Principles of Supervision (Fall CRN 87906)
  • TECH 4460 – Work Design / Improvement
  • TECH 4462 – Quality Improvement (Fall CRN 96334)
  • TECH 4464 – Production Control Systems

Networking and Security

Certificate Program

Learn essential skills in operating systems, networking technologies, and cybersecurity. This certificate prepares students for the Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA) certification.

Career Opportunities

  • Network Administrator
  • Technical Customer Support
  • Certified Network Technician

Courses

  • TECH 3241 – Internet Technology (Fall CRN 93374)
  • TECH 4272 – Operating Systems
  • TECH 42XX – Computer Network Technology
  • TECH 4282 – Enterprise Networking and Security

Degree & Certificate Programs

Beginning in Fall 2026, Polytechnic@UofM will offer workforce-aligned degree and certificate programs that combine condensed curricula with flexible pathways and applied learning.

All programs are designed to recognize transfer credit, industry certifications, and documented experience, helping students move more efficiently toward workforce-ready qualifications.

Degree Programs

B.S. in Information Technology

Degree Program

Focus on networking, cloud infrastructure, systems administration, and security fundamentals to support modern digital operations.

Planned launch: Fall 2026. Curriculum details will be published on the official University of Memphis program page.

B.S. in Manufacturing Supervision

Degree Program

Emphasis on leadership, quality, safety, and production management skills needed in advanced manufacturing environments.

Planned launch: Fall 2026. Curriculum details will be published on the official University of Memphis program page.

B.S. in Construction Leadership

Degree Program

Courses in project management, cost control, safety, and coordination across construction trades and stakeholders.

Planned launch: Fall 2026. Curriculum details will be published on the official University of Memphis program page.

Certificate Programs

Certificate in Manufacturing Leadership

Certificate Program

Short, focused curriculum for supervisors and emerging leaders in manufacturing, emphasizing operations, communication, and safety.

Ideal for working professionals seeking a targeted credential that complements existing experience.

Certificate in Networking and Security

Certificate Program

Foundational courses in computer networking, security principles, and basic administration aligned with industry certifications.

Designed for students who want to strengthen their technical skills or prepare for further study in Information Technology.

How Polytechnic programs support students

Condensed, focused pathways

Polytechnic curricula concentrate on essential courses aligned with specific workforce needs, helping students complete credentials more efficiently.

Integration with other majors

Programs are designed so students can pair technical pathways with other majors, creating combinations that match their goals and interests.

A new pathway to workforce-ready degrees

Polytechnic@UofM, a new academic unit within the Herff College of Engineering, is launching in Fall 2026 to provide flexible, workforce-aligned programs that recognize transfer credit, certifications, and real-world experience.

Request information > Apply now >

Why Polytechnic@UofM?

  • Condensed 30–40 credit degree pathways
  • Alternative math options and flexible electives
  • Credit for transfers, certifications, and experience
  • Programs aligned with high-need industries

Student Success Stories

These examples illustrate the types of outcomes Polytechnic@UofM is designed to support. Replace these placeholders with real stories as students complete internships, projects, and jobs.

IT student, logistics internship

A student in the Information Technology program completes a networking and security internship with a Memphis logistics company, gaining hands-on experience with enterprise systems and cloud tools.

Outcome: full-time offer as an IT support specialist upon graduation.

Manufacturing supervision, working professional

A mid-career supervisor applies prior certifications and on-the-job training toward a Manufacturing Supervision degree, completing the program through flexible, condensed coursework.

Outcome: promotion to plant operations manager with an increased leadership role.

Construction leadership, community projects

A Construction Leadership student works on community-focused projects while taking Polytechnic courses in scheduling, safety, and project management.

Outcome: internship with a regional construction firm and a clear pathway to a project engineer role.

Built for workforce preparedness

Polytechnic@UofM programs are designed with direct input from employers in logistics, manufacturing, construction, and related industries. Coursework emphasizes applied skills that translate directly into the workplace.

Flexible pathways for diverse learners

Whether you are a first-time student, a transfer student, or a working professional, Polytechnic@UofM offers options to count what you already know and accelerate your progress toward a degree.
